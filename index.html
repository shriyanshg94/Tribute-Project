A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

The term "Missile Man of India" refers to Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Dr. Kalam was not only a prominent scientist but also a visionary leader known for his contributions to India's space and missile development programs.Dr. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He studied aeronautical engineering and played a crucial role in the development of India's space and missile technology. Dr. Kalam was associated with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).His notable achievements include leading the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which successfully launched the satellite Rohini into space in 1980. Dr. Kalam also played a pivotal role in the development of ballistic missile technology, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.The nickname "Missile Man of India" was given to Dr. Abdul Kalam in recognition of his significant contributions to the nation's missile development programs. Apart from his scientific accomplishments, Dr. Kalam was known for his inspirational speeches and his efforts to promote education, especially in the field of science and technology, among the youth of India.Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire generations, and he is remembered as one of India's most respected and beloved leaders.
